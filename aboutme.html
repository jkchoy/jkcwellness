who am i?

I Thought I'd Be a hairstylist

Cosmetology was not it for me

My journey into the wellness industry didn't begin exactly as planned. In high school, I thougtht I wanted to be a hairstylist and makeup artist and enrolled in the Cosmetology program at Honolulu Community College with that goal in mind. Although I quickly realized it wasn't the right fit, I was determined to finish what I started. Along the way, I discovered a genuine love for facials, waxing, nail care, and especially pedicures—services that allowed me to connect with clients in a more meaningful, hands-on way.

It Just Clicked

I didn't force it
—it naturally felt right

After graduating, I continued my education in the Esthetician program, where I developed a deeper understanding of skin health. It was during this time that I found myself most drawn to the massage portion of each treatment. Once I entered massage school, everything clicked—I knew I had found my true calling. Over the years, I've trained in a variety of modalities, with reflexology becoming a favorite, and have had the privilege of working in both luxury resort spas and intimate boutique settings across the island. Each experience shaped me into the therapist I am today.

I never planned to own a business...

i'm glad i did it

Starting my own business wasn't originally part of the plan, but it became one of the greatest blessings of my career. This allowed me to create customized, holistic treatments and expand into modalities such as cupping therapy, ear seeding, and energy work.
